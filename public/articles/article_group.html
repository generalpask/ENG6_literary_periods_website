What are the characteristics of this period?


The 20th century was a period where technological and scientific advancements were rapid, drastically changing society and its culture.

Modernism generally focuses more on the individual and their day to day lives rather than society as opposed to the previous Victorian literary period. Problems with society were often pointed out in the form of irony and satire. Most works were written in first person in order for people to get a better understanding of the characters in modernist literature. This meant that the entire thought processes of characters were penned.

The characters were often people who are going through challenging times but still manage to stay afloat.

Experimentation was another characteristic of the period. A lot of authors stopped using old approaches to writing. For example, poetry was now written in free verse instead of following a rhyme scheme as done previously.
